Mia Sara Biography

Mia Sara is an American actress who was born on June 19, 1967, in Brooklyn Heights, New York, the United States as Mia Sarapochiello. Mia made her debut starring as Princess Lili in the fantasy film Legend (1985) as well as had her breakthrough starring as Sloane Peterson in the comedy film Ferris Bueller’s Day Off (1986). Mia also portrayed Melissa Walker in the science fiction film Timecop (1994), which won her the Saturn Award for Best Supporting Actress.

    Mia Sara Career

    Mia’s debut role as Princess Lili in Ridley Scott’s 1985 fairy-tale film Legend. Playing the role of Ferris Bueller’s girlfriend, Sloane Peterson, in the year 1986 blockbuster film Ferris Bueller’s Day Off made her even more popular. Mia also appeared in the 1987 miniseries Queenie, a roman à clef on actress Merle Oberon, as well as 1992’s A Stranger Among Us, directed by Sidney Lumet.

    In 1994 Mia starred opposite Jean-Claude Van Damme in the blockbuster Timecop, for which she won the Saturn Award for Best Supporting Actress. Mia also played the part of Molly Connor in The Impossible Elephant (2001). Her television roles have included playing Annie Knox in the science fiction series Time Trax (1993–94) along with Dr. Harleen Quinzel in the short-lived WB Network series Birds of Prey.
