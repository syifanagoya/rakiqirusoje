Early Life and Family of Michael Jordan

Michael Jeffrey Jordan, aka MJ, was born on February 17, 1963, in the beautiful city of Brooklyn, New York, United States of America.

Jordan is the proud son of James R. Jordan Sr (father) and Deloris Jordan (mother). His father was a bank employee and equipment supervisor, while his mother was a bank teller.

However, Michael had a pretty easy childhood along with his four siblings, Larry Jordan, Deloris Jordan, Roslyn Jordan, and James Jordan Jr.

High School

His family moved to Wilmington, North Carolina, where Michael attended Emsley A. Laney High School. From an early age, Jordan was interested in physical games and athletics.

He played several games, including basketball, baseball, and football, during high school. Over those, he chooses to stay with basketball and tried for the Varsity basketball team.

Due to his short height, 5 feet, and 11 inches, at that time, only his tall friend, Harvest Leroy Smith, was approved to play.

Jordan kept on trying hard and became the star player on Laney’s Junior Varsity team. Magical Jordan grew up four inches in the following year and earned a spot on the Varsity roster.

Likewise, during his senior year, he played in the 1981 McDonald’s All-American Game with an average score of 27 points.

Due to his amazing records, many colleges approached him, such as Duke, South Carolina, North Carolina, Virginia, Syracuse, etc.

Similarly, Michael chose the University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill and began his college domination.

College Career

Michael was recruited by coach Dean Smith’s team and named the ACC Freshman of the Year.

His jump shot against Georgetown was an epic that got the victory in the 1982 NCAA Championship game.

Jordan was the player that every team wanted after his victory shot and college career, with an average of 17.7 ppg on 54% shooting.

During his sophomore and junior season, he was selected to the NCAA-All-American First Team.

Jordan won several awards, such as Naismith and the Wooden College Player of the Year.

This Legendary basketball player got graduated in 1986 with a Bachelor of Arts degree in geography.

Professional Career

Early NBA Years

Professional Career Jordan began with his third pick in the 1984 NBA draft by the Chicago Bulls.

Jordan, during his rookie season, had an average of 28.2 ppg on 51.5% shooting.

After his presence in the game, the team won 35% of the games in three seasons. With his amazing gameplay and skills, the fans of the opposing team began to admire Jordan.

He began to appear on the cover of Sports Illustrated as “A Star Is Born.” Nonetheless, Jordan became the rookie player of the season.

Due to his excessive popularity, his team players were upset and even refused to pass the ball to Jordan.

However, he was named the Rookie of the Year and finished the season with a loss to Milwaukee Bucks.

Similarly, with the rise of popularity, the shoes he wore during the auction game in August 2020 were sold for $615,000.

Sadly in the second season, Jordan broke his foot and missed 64 games. With the absence of Jordan, the Chicago bulls had to suffer a loss and had the worst season in NBA history.

After his return to the game, he set the record of 63 points in a playoff game in two Games, which is still unbroken.

With his complete recovery and back in form, he had one of the most prolific seasons in NBA history.

Michael Jordan scored 3,000 points in a season and became the only player with an average of 37.1 points on 48.2% shooting.

Moreover, Jordan became the first player to record 200 steals and 100 blocked shots in one season.

National Team Career
